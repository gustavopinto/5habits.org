1+5 Habits for Young Researchers

Academia could be a wonderful place. By seeking the truth, researchers are exposed to several intellectual challenges, and their discoveries could shape the way we think, work, and live.

However, academia can also be a tough place. Graduate students are six times more likely to be depressed than the general population. The academic job market is extremely competitive. The pressure to publish could lead to frustration and anxiety.

If you happen to think that academic life is for you, it is better to foster some good habits.

Why this book?

Hi ðŸ‘‹ This is Gustavo Pinto!

I started my research career as a Computer Science Ph.D. student in 2011. At that time, my English was terrible and, as most of the students, I also stuggled to find a research topic and to write anything meaninful in a paper. During the first two years, I did not published any research paper related to my thesis. But I eventually found a topic I was excited about and the research papers started to get published. Fasting forward 10 years, I traveled the world, gave many academic talks at conferences, grew my collaboration network, achieved good academic metrics (you name it), and met hundreds of researchers.

What I made to achieve all of these things?

A the end of 2019, I was invited to give a talk at a Brazilian public university. I was supposed to talk about how to be productive in research. Young researchers, that is, master and Ph.D. students, working towards their degrees, mostly composed the audience of the talk.

I receive this invitation with great consternation but with great joy. The impostor inside me doubted that I could talk anything useful about research productivity. However, preparing that talk allowed me to stop and reflect a bit on my research career and, eventually, understand what and how I could improve my work. I then eventually realized "1+5 habits" that I believe are of great importance to my research work.

In this book I synthesize everything I know about good habits that could impact your research in a positive way.

Who This Book Is For?

This book is for young researchers. By young research, I meean anyone who is still trying to find her path in academia. It doesn't matter if you're a undergrad or postdoc. If you want to learn new habits or rethink existing behaviors, this book is for you.
